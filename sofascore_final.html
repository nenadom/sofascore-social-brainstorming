Palmer's Last-Gasp Thriller Sends Stamford Bridge Wild

Upcoming tab — content structure
1

Daily Brief header

Name, date, and three quick tags that tell the fan what's inside before they scroll. "2 games today · 3 transfer updates · 1 story breaking" — they know in one second whether it's worth reading.

2

Feature match card — "Why this game matters"

Not just kickoff time and teams. Four bullet points of stakes, form context, and key storylines. Palmer's derby record. Saka's doubt. The points gap. Turns a fixture into something you're emotionally prepared for.

3

Transfer radar — Rumour / Likely / Confirmed

Three confidence tiers remove the noise. Gyökeres to Arsenal is Likely. Haaland extension is Confirmed. No need to go to Twitter to calibrate credibility — SofaScore does it for you.

4

Drama / Locker room

Short, punchy, sourced. Ten Hag losing the dressing room. Mbappé furious after being benched. The content fans currently check Twitter for at 8am — positioned here, in context, before they scroll away.

5

Squad news — your teams only

Injury and availability color-coded: green / amber / red. Saka's doubt appears on Arsenal's row because Chelsea fans need that information too — it directly affects tonight's game they're following.

Social interaction layer
6

Rivalry Flashback

Historical stat surfaced as a bold narrative card. Chelsea's 7-game home unbeaten run against Arsenal is a real stat — framed as a story, not a table. Anchors the upcoming match in history and builds anticipation.

7

Hot Take — agree / disagree

Lowest-friction social entry point: one tap to register an opinion. Shows live vote percentages immediately — turns passive reading into active participation. Users see their view reflected in community data instantly.

8

Opinion Poll — 3-option vote

Chelsea win / Draw / Arsenal win — reveals community sentiment with animated fill bars on vote. The "who do you think wins" question is what fans say to each other at work. We surface it inside the app, before the game.

9

Prediction picker — 7 types

Tapping any prediction type expands an inline sub-card with contextual pill options (team names, player names, score options). "Post Prediction" commits the pick — we notify them at kickoff. Drives return engagement the moment the game starts.
