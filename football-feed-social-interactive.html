⚽ Moment Reactions

Each key event card carries a reaction strip — five curated emojis plus a custom option. Counts update live in real time. Fans can see the emotional wave of the community as a goal goes in. Tapping twice removes your reaction.

Live Post-game

🗳️ Live Poll — Vote to see results

Polls surface automatically at natural game moments: kickoff, half-time, big events, final whistle. Voting reveals the community result — your vote changes the live counts in real time. Polls are ephemeral: they expire and archive after the game.

Live Post-game

🌡️ Fan Pulse — Community sentiment

A sliding confidence meter fans update throughout the game. The aggregate position shifts as the match evolves — a goal in the 90th minute sends it flying right. Great for editorial storytelling post-match: "Fan confidence hit its lowest point at 2–0 down."

Live only

🔥 Debate Cards — Instant hot takes

Auto-generated after controversial moments (VAR overturned, red card, dubious penalty). Single-tap Agree/Disagree with instant result reveal. The simplicity makes it frictionless — no typing, maximum engagement at moments of peak emotion.

Live Post-game

🔮 Predictions + 🏆 Leaderboard

Pre-match score and scorer predictions, locked at kickoff. Points awarded for correct outcomes, bonus for exact scores. A persistent Prediction League with weekly and season standings creates long-term retention — fans return game after game to protect their rank and their streak.

Social Design Principles

Rules that keep the community healthy and fun

Social features can go wrong fast. These principles prevent that.

01

One-tap minimum effort

Every social interaction must be completable in one tap. No text required to participate. Football is fast — if voting takes more than a second, fans won't bother at the moment that counts.

02

Results as reward

Never hide the community result until after the fan has participated. Show them what everyone else thought — that's the payoff. The reveal is the dopamine hit that brings them back next time.

03

Context-anchored, not free-form

No global chat. Social interactions attach to specific cards and moments. This keeps conversations focused, prevents toxicity, and makes every reaction meaningful rather than noise.

04

Feed learns from your behaviour

What you react to, vote on, and predict trains the feed's understanding of your depth. A fan who only hits reactions stays casual. A fan who uses all features gets deeper content surfaced automatically.

05

Ephemeral live, persistent legacy

Live interactions (pulse, live polls) disappear after the game — they're of the moment. But predictions, ratings, and leaderboard scores persist and build a personal history that gives fans something to own long-term.

06

Community size as signal, not noise

Show fan counts and reaction volumes — "84k watching", "3.2k 🔥" — as social proof. Seeing others engaged makes any individual feel part of something bigger than their screen. Scale is the product.
