☀️ Banner — Identity & Trust

The "Morning Digest" header is a consistent, daily ritual anchor. The date and "For you" badge signal this card is personal, not generic. Fans should recognise it instantly every morning.

Always pinned as the very first card in the feed
Disappears and reshapes as "Evening Recap" after 18:00
Amber colour language = informational, not urgent
2

🌙 Last Night — What you missed

Tappable result rows ordered by relevance: followed teams first, then biggest games. Each result links straight to the full match report. Your team's result always appears first and is visually distinguished.

Max 3 results shown inline — more behind expand
Win/draw/loss colour coded on the score
If no games last night, section replaced with "Yesterday's top story"
3

📅 Today — What's coming

Today's fixtures with kickoff times and a one-tap notification bell. The most important match (biggest rivalry, your followed team, or top league billing) is highlighted. The bell icon turns on/off instantly — no confirmation screen.

Kickoff time shown in user's local timezone always
"Soon" green colour fires when game is <2 hrs away
Max 3 fixtures shown — tapping "Today" tab shows full list
4

🔥 Trending Now — The one story to know

A single curated story — injury, transfer rumour, controversy, record broken. One story only: enough to intrigue, not enough to overwhelm. Tapping opens the full article or relevant feed section. Editorially curated but AI-assisted for relevance.

Prioritises stories that affect today's games first
Injury news affecting today's fixture = always top
Source + timestamp shown for trust and freshness
5

▾ Expand — For the curious

The collapsed section holds yesterday's aggregate numbers (goals, cards, penalties) and a second trending story. Hidden by default to keep the card compact — die-hards expand it, casuals don't need to.

Remembers expand state: if you always open it, it opens by default
Transfer pulse slot: only shows if a real story broke overnight
Aggregate stats make fans feel informed in 2 seconds

Personalization Logic

How the card knows what you care about

Three layers of signals build the right digest for every fan.

⭐

Explicit follows

Teams and leagues the fan has explicitly followed always appear first — their result, their fixture, their injury news. This is the foundation layer. If you follow Man United, their result leads every morning, win or lose.

👁

Implicit behaviour

Which match pages they open, which player ratings they check, which stories they read. If a fan opens every Liverpool match report even without following them, Liverpool content surfaces in their digest automatically.

🗓

Fan depth signal

Casual fans (few opens, reaction-only) get a compact 3-section card with big clear results. Die-hards (daily use, expand often, prediction players) get the expanded card open by default with deeper stats and more stories.
